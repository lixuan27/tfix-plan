08  the system

The pipeline in full. Click a stage.

The editor stays frozen stage a

An image editor runs on every frame at four distilled steps. It keeps each car, sign and pole in place, which is the precondition for everything downstream. Its seed policy is the dial that matters.

A shared latent space frozen

A video codec and a text encoder. We measured the codec floor first and found it clean, so the flicker in the draft is genuinely ours to remove.

The fixer is the one trained model stage b

A causal video transformer, started from a distilled streaming generator. The draft and the source enter through fresh input channels that begin at zero, so training starts exactly at the pretrained model and moves forward from there.

The runtime streams deployment

Chunk by chunk, with a rolling cache of fixed size whose positions are re-indexed to stay inside the trained range. Any stream length, constant memory, constant latency.

The output is watched, then measured rule

Blur, frozen motion and a reverted edit all please a flicker metric. So the video decides first and the table confirms afterwards.

10  how much noise to teach

The reconstruction gives the right kind of flicker. We add the right amount.

A reconstruction flickers the same way a real edit does, in spectrum and in colour, at about a third of the amount. Jitter that leaves appearance untouched covers the rest, so the clean clip stays the right answer.

the trap

A stronger prompt reaches the same amount by repainting the scene, and then the clean clip answers a different question. So we move the amount while appearance holds still.

12  the plan

Five stages, each carrying a number it must reach. Click one.

Calibrate the frozen editor

Probe it with zero edit, sweep the seed policy, and measure both the amount and the kind of flicker. Gate reached: draft flicker 9.4, down from 48.4, with structure intact.

Fallback in hand: couple the noise to optical flow so it follows the content.

Measure the train and test gap

Build identity, mild and drastic drafts on the same clips and compare their flicker spectra. Gate reached: the same kind of flicker, at about a third of the amount.

Fallback in hand: blend in real mild edit drafts with a consensus target.

Train the fixer running now

Three arms on real reconstruction pairs, each trained with the source withheld. Checkpoints arrive every five hundred steps. Gate: the flicker falls by a third or more, and the edit survives, judged on watched video.

Fallback in hand: move the target to a styled clip, so preserving the edit becomes the only way to satisfy the loss.

Driving domain, then the long horizon

Rebuild the reconstruction corpus on driving footage, retrain the winning arm, attach the rolling cache, and trace the drift across minute long streams. Gate: metrics hold on driving, latency stays flat with length.

Driving scenes are rigid, and flicker grows with organic texture, so the domain favours us.

The head to head

Two editing benchmarks plus driving footage, with the competitor checkpoint run inside our own harness. Gate: temporal metrics beside a dedicated edit supervised editor, while we keep more motion.

Per frame sharpness follows the editor. We raise it at the front end, or we state it plainly.
