The premise, in one clip

Content is solved. Time is not.

Frozen image editor

Structure-exact. It cannot drift, because it has no memory.

Its one defect

Every frame is drawn independently, so the sequence flickers.

This single observation defines the whole project: do not teach a video model to edit — teach it to repair what an image model already did.

1 · Task

Compose two pretrained models. Learn only what is left.

Given a video and an instruction, produce an edited video that is faithful on every frame and consistent across frames — causally, at constant cost. We never learn that map. We factor it.

One trained component. Everything else is pretrained and frozen.03 / 15
1 · Task — why the factorization pays

Editing is a hard question. Deflickering is an easy one.

Once the image model has committed to what the scene should look like, almost nothing is left to decide.

The residual is a small, structured, frame-to-frame correction — and a small correction can be learned from clean video alone, with no edited video anywhere in the training set.

2 · Challenge

Why nobody gets this for free.

Click a route to see what it costs.

It needs edited video to learn from — and it drifts.

Edit pairs must be synthesized by a larger model and filtered by a VLM; bad pairs actively hurt the fine-tune. Worse, editing now lives inside the recurrent state, so it drifts over minutes — and the known fixes repair the base rollout, not the edit path.

Measured on our videos: the leading open streaming editor reaches motion smoothness 0.993 — with dynamic degree 0.25. A near-static video is trivially consistent.

It cannot stream.

These methods need keyframes or the whole clip, so they are non-causal by construction — and their notion of consistency is hand-designed rather than learned.

It cannot tell an edit from an artifact.

Optical-flow smoothing removes 43% of the flicker, and blurs the frame while erasing the edit along with it (edit preservation 0.80 → 0.75). Anything that only averages will average away the thing you asked for.

Every route pays with either data, causality, or the edit itself.05 / 15
2 · Challenge — what we hit ourselves

Two walls. Both structural, neither a tuning problem.

Wall 1 · the frontier

Every fixer-side idea slides along one curve.

The 10×-capacity "gain" was reproduced exactly by a zero-training pixel blend of the small model. Capacity bought nothing.

Wall 2 · the shortcut

Train on clean video carelessly, and the fixer undoes the edit.

If the clean source is both the target and an input, "copy the source" is the cheapest way to be temporally consistent.

Wall 1 was solved by leaving the fixer alone. Wall 2 by removing the shortcut.06 / 15
3 · Insight — where the flicker actually comes from

We were manufacturing most of the flicker ourselves.

A probe: run the editor with no edit at all, then change only the noise seed policy.

flicker 26.2

Zero edit · shared seed6.96

The edit contributes almost nothing. The independent noise draw was the flicker. Fixing the protocol — zero training — took the real draft from 48.4 to 9.4 and beat our entire previous system.

Inspiration: noise is a latent variable, and we had been resampling it 33 times per clip.07 / 15
3 · Insight — the constraint was the system

The two stages must be calibrated to each other.

Five single-sided interventions failed because the ceiling was never in the fixer's loss.

It was in an un-calibrated pair of stages: an editor left noisier than it needed to be, and a fixer trained for a flicker level that no longer existed.

Diagnose the frozen model first. Then, and only then, retrain the learned one against what the frozen one actually does.

This is the project's real contribution — a strategy, not a loss term.08 / 15

4 · New evaluation

Three ways to fake a fix. We built the instruments that catch them.

Blur it

Softening the frame lowers flicker.

Freeze it

A static video is trivially "consistent".

Revert it

The unedited source never flickers.

New metric

Source-anchored structure

Flow taken from the source, features style-invariant, floor set by the source against itself. It cannot be gamed by the output's own jitter.

New measurement

Flicker character

Not just how much flicker, but what kind. Turns "does my training noise match my test noise?" into a measurable claim.

Protocol fix

Motion beside smoothness

Smoothness is never reported alone. A smoothness win with collapsed motion is disqualified.

Hard gate

Anti-reversion

Edit preservation on every run. And the rule that caught the failure: watch the video before reading the table.

The instruments falsified two of our own claims. That is what they are for.10 / 15
5 · Pipeline — click any stage

The full system.

Stage A — the editor frozen

Qwen-Image-Edit, 4-step distilled. Structure-exact: every car, sign and pole stays in place — that is the precondition for everything downstream. Its seed policy is the one dial that matters; guidance turned out to be a no-op here.

2.2 s/frame at 720p, against 37 s/frame for the 40-step non-distilled model, at nearly the same sharpness.

Shared latent space frozen

Wan video VAE and a text encoder, both frozen. We measured the codec's own floor before trusting anything in latent space: its round-trip flicker is 1.08 against a natural-video floor of 1.05 — it neither adds nor hides flicker, so the ~9 units of draft flicker are genuinely ours to remove.

Stage B — the fixer trained

A causal video diffusion transformer, initialized from a distilled streaming generator. The draft and the source are concatenated into its input channels, with the new channels zero-initialized — so training begins exactly at the pretrained model and can only improve from there.

1.3B parameters. A 10× larger version gave no genuine gain; capacity is not the missing ingredient.

Streaming runtime

Chunk by chunk, with a fixed-size rolling cache whose positions are re-indexed so they never leave the range the model was trained on. Arbitrary stream length, constant memory and latency, nothing precomputed.

Instruments frozen

Flow, style-invariant structure features, edit-preservation and a VLM judge — all off-the-shelf, all re-run by us on identical videos. Competitor checkpoints are executed in our harness, not quoted.

Frozen = dashed. Trained = filled.11 / 15
6 · Algorithm

Start at the draft, not at noise.

The video model is asked to travel a very short distance — from a nearly-correct draft to its consistent version.

Anchored to the draft

A penalty holds the output at the draft's appearance. It may change time. It may not change looks.

Trained without the source

The clean source is withheld from the input, so "copy the source" is not available — the shortcut that reverted the edit.

One step vs four, flicker removed56.9% / 56.1%

7 · The training-data engine

Clean video in. Training pairs out.

The reconstruction gives the same kind of flicker as a real edit — same temporal spectrum, same colour balance — but only a third of the amount. A stronger prompt would close the gap and destroy the target, because it changes the appearance. So we close it with appearance-preserving jitter instead.

The trap we avoid

The only prompt that reaches the target amplitude also repaints the scene — and then the clean video is no longer the right answer. Amplitude is a knob we must turn without touching appearance.

No instruction, no synthesized edit pair, no VLM filter — any editor can produce this.13 / 15
8 · The plan — click a stage for its gate and its fallback

Five stages. Each one has a number it must hit.

Stage 0 · Calibrate the frozen editor

Probe it with no edit. Sweep the seed policy, the step count, the guidance. Measure both the amount and the kind of flicker it produces. Confirm by eye that structure is exact.

GATE — PASSED

Draft flicker at or below 10 · got 9.40, down from 48.4. Structure-exact confirmed. Editor runs at 2.2 s/frame.

IF IT HAD FAILED

Couple the noise to the optical flow, so it follows the content instead of the frame index.

Stage 1 · Quantify the train–test gap

Generate identity, mild and drastic drafts on the same clips. Compare their flicker spectra. Decide what the fixer should be trained on — the question our mentor asked first.

GATE — PASSED

Same kind of flicker confirmed (33% vs 34% high-frequency share). Amplitude gap measured: 2.07 vs 7.37. A 16-cell sweep proved prompts cannot close it safely.

RESIDUAL RISK

If jitter under-mimics the editor, mix in a small set of real mild-edit drafts with a consensus target.

Stage 2 · Train the fixer — source-free, draft-anchored

Three arms on real reconstruction pairs, all trained without the clean source in the input: a balanced anchor, a stronger appearance anchor, and a more faithful, less regenerative variant. Checkpoints every 500 steps; the model saturates near 1000.

GATE — BOTH, OR NEITHER

Flicker down by ≥30% on real edit drafts and edit preservation ≥0.93 — judged on watched video. A night→day edit must still be daytime.

IF ALL THREE REVERT

Switch the target from the clean source to a styled one, so target ≠ source and reversion becomes impossible by construction.

Stage 3 · Driving domain, then long horizon

Rebuild the reconstruction corpus on driving footage and retrain the winning arm. Attach the rolling cache. Measure the drift curve over minute-long streams and the per-chunk latency.

GATE

Metrics hold on driving clips · drift slope below a third of an end-to-end editor's · latency flat with stream length.

WHY WE EXPECT IT

Flicker scales with organic texture. Driving scenes are rigid — a favourable domain.

Stage 4 · The head-to-head

Two editing benchmarks plus driving footage. The competitor's checkpoint is run by us, on our videos, in our harness. Smoothness is never reported without motion beside it.

GATE

Temporal metrics near a dedicated edit-supervised editor while preserving more motion — at zero paired data, one step, and no retraining when the editor changes.

THE BOUNDARY WE WILL NOT HIDE

Per-frame sharpness is capped by the editor's re-render. We improve it at the front end, or we state it.

No stage starts before the previous one passes. Failing arms are cancelled at the first checkpoint.14 / 15
