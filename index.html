Streaming video editing

How do we edit a live video stream without drift, from a temporal denoising point of view?

What

Restyle a video as it arrives, chunk by chunk, for minutes at a time.

Why it is hard

Editing inside an autoregressive video model accumulates error, so the picture drifts away from the scene.

Our angle

Recast the task as flicker denoising and temporal reconstruction, and let pretrained models carry the rest.

scroll

01   why this problem is worth solving

Every live pixel pipeline asks for the same three things at once.

The core problem: land one instruction on every frame as it arrives, and hold the frames in agreement with one another, for a stream of unbounded length.

02   how the field solves it today

Distil a video editor into a four step causal student.

It buys

Real time speed, and a genuinely causal editor.

It costs

Edited video pairs, synthesised by a larger model and filtered before use.

It keeps

The edit inside the recurrent state, which is exactly where drift is born.

04   the limit of the state of the art

Current streaming editors deliver a part of the task.

What the videos show

Stability is purchased with editing strength

A distilled causal editor keeps the sequence coherent by remaining close to the source, and the instruction weakens in exchange. The two goals are competing inside a single model.

The question it raises

Which model should carry the edit?

Image models already follow an instruction with full strength on a single frame. The difficulty appears only when a recurrent video model is asked to carry that instruction through time.

05   the diagnosis

Drift is the price of putting the edit inside the recurrence.

06   the idea

The image model edits. The video model fixes.

Six consecutive frames of one real per frame edit. Every car, sign and pole holds its place.
